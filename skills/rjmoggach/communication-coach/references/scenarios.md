# Practice Scenarios

Organized by dimension and difficulty level. User receives one scenario, completes it, gets scored.

---

## CLARITY

### Level 1: Basic Simplification

**Scenario:** You receive this message from a colleague:
> "In consideration of the fact that we are currently experiencing a substantial degree of uncertainty regarding the timeline associated with the deliverable in question, I wanted to reach out to see if there might be an opportunity to engage in a dialogue about potentially adjusting our expectations."

**Task:** Rewrite this in under 30 words. Keep the core request.

**Scoring Focus:** Sentence length, jargon removal, directness

---

### Level 2: Structure Under Pressure

**Scenario:** You have 60 seconds to explain why your team missed a deadline. The CEO is waiting.

**Task:** Write exactly 3 sentences:
1. What happened
2. Why it happened  
3. What's next

**Scoring Focus:** Structural markers, clarity, no hedging

---

### Level 3: Complex Simplification

**Scenario:** Explain how OAuth 2.0 works to a non-technical executive who needs to approve the implementation.

**Task:** 150 words max. No jargon. Make it matter.

**Scoring Focus:** Jargon density, analogies, clarity, persuasion integration

---

## VOCAL CONTROL

### Level 1: Filler Elimination

**Scenario:** You're inviting someone to coffee.

**Task:** Write the invitation in under 20 words. No filler words allowed.

**Prohibited:** like, just, maybe, kind of, sort of, actually, basically, um, you know

**Scoring Focus:** Filler count (must be zero)

---

### Level 2: Hedging Removal

**Scenario:** You've decided to change careers. Tell your mentor.

**Task:** Announce your decision in 2-3 sentences. No hedging.

**Prohibited:** might, maybe, probably, I think, considering, exploring, hoping

**Scoring Focus:** Confidence markers, declarative statements

---

### Level 3: High-Stakes Delivery

**Scenario:** You're pitching your startup to investors. You have 30 seconds for the hook.

**Task:** Write your opening. It must create urgency and clarity.

**Scoring Focus:** Strong verbs, no fillers, rhythm, punch

---

## PRESENCE

### Level 1: Simple Assertion

**Scenario:** Someone asks if you can work this weekend. You can't and don't want to.

**Task:** Decline in one sentence. No questions, no justification.

**Scoring Focus:** Declarative form, no weak language

---

### Level 2: Ownership Under Scrutiny

**Scenario:** Your decision led to a failed experiment. Your boss asks what happened.

**Task:** Explain in 3 sentences using first-person ("I decided..."). Own it completely.

**Scoring Focus:** First-person ownership, no deflection, no minimizing

---

### Level 3: Authority in Ambiguity

**Scenario:** Two teammates disagree on technical direction. Both are looking to you to decide. You don't have perfect information.

**Task:** Make the call in 4 sentences:
1. The decision
2. The reasoning
3. What you're watching for
4. How you'll adjust if needed

**Scoring Focus:** Decisiveness despite uncertainty, clear reasoning, confidence

---

## PERSUASION

### Level 1: Basic Ethos/Pathos/Logos

**Scenario:** Convince your manager to approve remote work one day per week.

**Task:** Write a 100-word pitch including:
- One credibility signal (ethos)
- One stakes/benefit statement (pathos)
- One data point or logical argument (logos)

**Scoring Focus:** All three elements present and clear

---

### Level 2: Stakeholder Mapping

**Scenario:** You need budget approval from three people: CFO (cares about ROI), CTO (cares about risk), VP Product (cares about speed).

**Task:** Write a 50-word pitch that addresses all three concerns without naming them explicitly.

**Scoring Focus:** Multi-stakeholder persuasion, economy of language

---

### Level 3: Hostile Audience

**Scenario:** You're proposing a process change that will make your colleagues' lives harder in the short term for long-term gain. They're skeptical and annoyed.

**Task:** Write a 200-word message that:
- Acknowledges their pain directly
- Makes the case for change
- Offers a path that respects their concerns
- Ends with a clear ask

**Scoring Focus:** Empathy + persuasion, handling objections, CTA

---

## BOUNDARY SETTING

### Level 1: Direct No

**Scenario:** A friend asks you to help them move this Saturday. You don't want to.

**Task:** Decline in under 15 words. No apology, no justification.

**Scoring Focus:** Directness, no apology, completeness

---

### Level 2: Professional Boundary

**Scenario:** A colleague keeps scheduling meetings during your blocked focus time.

**Task:** Set the boundary in 2-3 sentences. Be clear, be kind, offer alternative.

**Scoring Focus:** Clarity, no apology, solution-oriented

---

### Level 3: High-Pressure Boundary

**Scenario:** Your boss emails at 9pm asking for a deliverable by 8am tomorrow. It's unreasonable and you've set work-life boundaries before.

**Task:** Respond tonight. Hold the boundary, offer alternative timeline, maintain relationship.

**Scoring Focus:** Firmness under pressure, no guilt, professionalism, alternative offered

---

## EMOTIONAL REGULATION

### Level 1: Calm Disagreement

**Scenario:** Someone criticizes your work in a meeting. You disagree with their assessment.

**Task:** Respond in 2 sentences. Acknowledge their view, state yours. No defensiveness.

**Scoring Focus:** Neutral tone, clarity, no emotional language

---

### Level 2: Frustrated But Professional

**Scenario:** This is the third time this teammate has missed a deadline, blocking your work.

**Task:** Address it in 3-4 sentences. State the pattern, the impact, the ask. Stay factual.

**Scoring Focus:** Factual vs. emotional language, directness, solution focus

---

### Level 3: Conflict Resolution

**Scenario:** Two people are arguing in a meeting you're leading. It's getting personal.

**Task:** Intervene in 2-3 sentences to:
1. Acknowledge both perspectives
2. Redirect to productive ground
3. Move forward

**Scoring Focus:** De-escalation, authority, forward motion
